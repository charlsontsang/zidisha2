Burkina Faso
Siaka Traore

Siaka lives in a tiny village in the vast interior of the desert country of Burkina Faso. The nearest source of running water and electricity is fifteen kilometers away – a full day’s journey by donkey cart. Siaka used Zidisha loans to turn a dusty wasteland into a thriving cassava farm and processing factory - employing dozens and generating profits that have transformed the prospects of his entire community.

Senegal
Aissatou Traore

Aissatou is an intrepid saleslady from the war-torn Casamance in West Africa, who dreams of making enough money to send her son to college overseas. Inspired by Dale Carnegie's idea that there is a "mine of gold" inside each of us, she gets up at four in the morning to read business books before heading out into the bush on sales trips.

Kenya
Paul Kamande

Paul Kamande is an Electrical Engineering student in Kenya and quite a remarkable person. At age twenty-three, he designed an automated irrigation system adapted to the unique climate of his home village in central Kenya. Thanks to technology, poverty didn't stop him from going to college: he used a combination of Zidisha loans and an Indiegogo campaign to fund his tuition.
